About Me

My research creates the connective tissue between raw data and human experience. With a foundation in Electrical and Computer Engineering from USC’s SAIL (Signal Analysis and Interpretation Laboratory), I spent years decoding the subtleties of human communication—how we speak, move, and emote.

Today, I apply those insights at the scale of the internet. At Google DeepMind, I lead efforts in generative media and agentic AI, moving beyond static analysis to build systems that can synthesize complex video and audio narratives. I don't just teach machines to see; I teach them to understand context, culture, and causality.

Research Philosophy

Perception Meets Creation

My technical agenda focuses on the convergence of perception and creation. I architect Multimodal Generative AI systems that go beyond simple text prompts to synthesize video and audio with cinematic coherence and temporal consistency.

However, generation without understanding is insufficient. That is why I ground my models in Computational Media Intelligence—applying rigorous analysis to how AI interprets human behavior, emotion, and social bias. From parsing box-office films for representation to training large models on physical dynamics, my goal is to build intelligence that is not only powerful but sociologically aware.
