About Me

I build robust machine intelligence models by learning from multiple views and modalities in datasets. My research focuses on multimodal machine learning, bridging the gap between raw signals and human-level understanding.

Currently, I am a Senior Research Engineer at Google DeepMind in New York City, working on next-generation generative models. I enjoy working on open-ended problems that contribute to products with a tangible societal impact.

Prior to DeepMind, I completed my PhD at USC's Signal Analysis and Interpretation Laboratory (SAIL), where I developed inclusive technologies to analyze media content and computational imaging.

Research Areas

Multimodal Intelligence & Society

Generative Media (VideoPoet & Gemini): Pushing the frontier of zero-shot video generation and agentic capabilities. My work leverages large language models to synthesize high-fidelity motion and audio, creating systems that understand temporal dynamics and consistency.

Computational Media Intelligence: I develop frameworks to "watch" and "listen" to media at scale. This involves analyzing character representations, quantifying social bias in entertainment, and using AI to decode complex human interactions.
